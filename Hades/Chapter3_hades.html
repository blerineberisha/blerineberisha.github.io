Chapter III

I’m standing on the balcony, reviewing everything I heard in the interview when I suddenly hear steps. “Tell me, Zeus”, I hear Athena say. “Why did you leave your lightning in the main palace in the first place? Don’t you usually take it with you?”, she asked. Zeus thinks for a second and then answers. “Yes, but I was… otherwise distracted that night and had to leave in a hurry.” “But you still locked the door?”, Athena asks. “Well, yes. I’m very used to locking my door, even in a hurry”, Zeus replies. “To be honest, I’m still confused as to why you’re investigating when I hired someone myself”, he continues. Athena takes a deep breath. “You know I like to take matters into my own hands. I don’t know why you didn’t come to me in the first place, I’m by far the most capable.” “You’re the smartest, the wisest, I’ll let you have that-”, Zeus starts but Athena interrupts. “You don’t trust me.” She shakes her head. “You lost your lightning, other Gods are starting to question your authority.. And you’re in over your head.” I can see Zeus getting angry. “Who is questioning my authority?”, he demands. “See?”, Athena asks. “Your lightning is gone, yet you’re ready to fight everyone who might just have one negative thought about you”, she sighs. “Whoever is questioning my authority might be the thief!” “Is that what you truly believe”, Athena asks. “Or do you just want to give them a more severe punishment?” Athena asks with her most empathetic voice, but I can see a tiny smirk forming in her face.
