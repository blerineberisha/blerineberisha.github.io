Hermes

“So what were you doing again? You said you saw Aphrodite and Ares at the main palace, but what were you doing there?”, I ask. “I was… delivering a little something for Zeus”, Hermes answers. “Ah, yes, I remember”, I say. “So what were you delivering, though?” He starts to get nervous. “I’m not sure if I’m allowed to tell you, to be honest”, he says. “Well, since it’s about a missing lightning, I think it’s a good idea to tell me.” Hermes hesitates. “I won’t even mention it in the files”, I add reassuringly. “Alrighty”, Hermes sighs. “I was delivering a Hermès bag for Hera… Zeus kind of might’ve cheated on her again and he needed something to apologize with. I delivered it to her room in the main palace and then wanted to report to Zeus. When I saw that he wasn’t in the main palace, I went to look for him. Until I found him with another mortal…”, he scratches his neck. “Not exactly a picture I want to recall”, he adds with a nervous yet disgusted look on his face. “Fair”, I say. “What did you do after that?”, I ask. “Oh, just wandered around a little. Did some parkour. Sparked some mischief in the mortal world. Wanna see what I stole? It’s quite fascinating-” “That’s quite alright. Thanks, Hermes. That’d be all I need”, I interrupt him, before he explodes.
