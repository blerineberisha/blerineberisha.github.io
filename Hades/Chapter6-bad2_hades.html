Chapter VI

You accuse Athena and you’re now standing in court, trying to prove that it’s the other one. “Do you think it’s believable he was in court all day? Documents can easily be faked, especially as the king of the underworld”, Athena explains. “I was able to clear every God and Goddess, but not our dearest Hades here.” “I don’t like having to accuse Hades”, Athena continues. “But since I was able to clear everyone, Hades’ cover story just seems the most unlikely to me”, she finishes. “And that is why I think that you should punish Hades for stealing the lightning. Thank you.” Now it’s my turn, but I choke on my own words. I can barely get a word out. I'm in shock. I can’t explain why – even if I had the words, I doubt I’d be able to say them right now – but I can neither defend myself nor explain why it’s Athena. Athena smiles a wicked smile. Game over! Oh no, the Olympians agreed with Athena and you were punished for it!
